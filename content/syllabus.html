Course syllabus

Part I — Foundations of psycholinguistics

  • Session 1: What is psycholinguistics? What do psycholinguists investigate? (Cutler, Klein & Levinson, 2005)

  • Session 2: Why and how to investigate linguistic meaning using psycholinguistic tools? (Noveck & Reboul, 2008)

  • Session 3: Investigating language production (Gibbs & Van Orden, 2012)

  • Session 4: Investigating language comprehension (Schumacher, 2018)

  • Session 5: Investigating the relation between production and comprehension (Gambi & Pickering, 2018)

  • Session 6: Modeling psycholinguistic data (Crocker, 2005)

Part II — Selected readings

  • Session 7: Production studies (Clarke, Elsner & Rohde, 2013; Domaneschi, Romero & Braun, 2017)

  • Session 8: Self-paced reading studies (Bergen & Grodner, 2012; Rubio-Fernandez, 2013)

  • Session 9: Visual world eye-tracking studies (Kurumada, Brown, Bibyk, Pontillo & Tanenhaus, 2014; van Bergen & Bosker, 2018)

  • Session 10: Mouse-tracking studies (Dale & Duran, 2011; Tomlinson, Bailey & Bott, 2013)

  • Session 11: Timing in turn-taking as a window into the production-comprehension interface (Levinson & Torreira, 2015, pp.10-24)

  • Session 12: Modeling production data (Herbstritt & Franke, 2019)

  • Session 13: Discourse, cognition and human interaction (Levinson, 2006)
