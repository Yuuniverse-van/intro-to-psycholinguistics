Course syllabus

Part I — Foundations of psycholinguistics

  • Session 1: What is psycholinguistics? What do psycholinguists investigate?

  • Session 2: Why, and how to, investigate linguistic meaning using psycholinguistic tools?

  • Session 3: Processes and models of language production

  • Session 4: Methods in language production research

  • Session 5: Processes and models of language comprehension

  • Session 6: Methods in language comprehension research pt. 1

  • Session 7: Methods in language comprehension research pt. 2

  • Session 8: Modeling psycholinguistic data

Part II — Selected readings

  • Session 9: Production studies (Clarke, Elsner & Rohde, 2013; Domaneschi, Romero & Braun, 2017)

  • Session 10: Self-paced reading studies (Bergen & Grodner, 2012; Rubio-Fernandez, 2013)

  • Session 11: Visual world eye-tracking studies (Kurumada, Brown, Bibyk, Pontillo & Tanenhaus, 2014; van Bergen & Bosker, 2018)

  • Session 12: Mouse-tracking studies (Dale & Duran, 2011; Tomlinson, Bailey & Bott, 2013)

  • Session 13: Modeling production data (Herbstritt & Franke, 2019)

  • Session 14: Discourse, cognition and human interaction (Levinson, 2006)
